Site Audit Pro

I joined Veam Studios, developers of Site Audit Pro, as the sole designer in 2012, at the time it was a one person operation working out of a living room. Throughout the products growth from a hobby project to a staple of the auditing and inspecting workflow, I have worked consistently to champion design-led thinking and development in all aspects of the product. Site Audit Pro is used by thousands of people everyday as an integral part of their workflow.

Starting Points

In its early life, Site Audit Pro struggled with a confusing interface, user experience was an afterthought. Upon joining, I worked to transform the product to focus on its users needs, ensuring that it was making work easier, not harder. It was challenging to work on an already established product, users already had ideas about how the app worked and the patterns for use, so it was essential that I didn’t simply start over fresh, but build on the foundations that were there from the beginning.

Understanding How It’s Used

Site Audit Pro is all about speeding up a process. Instead of lengthy pen and paper audits, users can quickly note and describe issues, then share a report of all their findings. Simplicity, not trying to do too much, was always the key to this apps success, so it was essential that this was never forgotten.

Functionality had to always be accessible, no matter the scenario. Boosting the size of tap targets for key actions afforded the user the ability to efficiently work in less than ideal conditions; pouring rain, blazing sunshine, inside a sewer, the list of possible use cases is almost endless.

Maintaining Simplicity

As the app has developed, new features have been designed and integrated; batch image importing, photo annotation and customisable report themes, to name a few. The challenge was to ensure the simplicity of the product remained, regardless of the added complexity of features. This challenge was met by observing and responding to common user behaviours, making sure the product works how they work. Working within learned and understood iOS patterns brought a familiar feel to the product, custom features were always designed to blend seamlessly into the experience.

New Platforms, Consistent Experience

Another challenge I faced on this project was its expansion to Android. This meant designing for a completely new set of software patterns, new user experiences and many different devices.

I had to approach the product from a slightly different angle than it’s iOS counterpart, but still ensuring a consistent user and brand experience. Through the use of consistent visual language and interactions, tailored for each operating system, the product retains its consistency across platforms. This gave me great experience working with a development agency, delivering prototypes and assets to ensure smooth delivery of the final product.

Learning By Doing

Throughout my time as the design lead working on Site Audit Pro, I have led 2 major iOS redesigns, numerous new feature integrations and an the entire design of it’s successful release on Android. I have learned an enormous amount from the first hand experience of developing a product with real users, users that rely on a product everyday. Working with a small development team has been challenging, but has allowed me a good understanding of how to prioritise product functionality and design. Learning to keep my aesthetic ego in check, focusing on real users and their needs instead, has been an eye opening and valuable experience. I have learned that small changes can have an enormous effect, and that what seems right to begin with must be studied, tested and iterated upon with a sensitive understanding of users; real life people.

You can find Site Audit Pro in the iOS App Store, and in the Android Play Store.
